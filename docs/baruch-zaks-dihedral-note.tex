Let
\[
  \mathcal A_{n-1}=\{x_i=x_j\}
  \subset
  \mathbb R^n/\langle(1,\ldots,1)\rangle
\]
be the type \(A_{n-1}\) braid arrangement. Its chambers are indexed by the
\(n!\) linear orders of the coordinates \(x_1,\ldots,x_n\).

There is a cyclic ordering of all chambers, obtained from Zaks' recursive
Hamiltonian-cycle algorithm, with the following property. If
\[
  \Phi_n:\{\text{chambers of }\mathcal A_{n-1}\}
  \longrightarrow
  \mathbb Z/n!\mathbb Z
\]
denotes this ordering, then the cyclic rotation of the indices
\[
  r:1\mapsto2\mapsto\cdots\mapsto n\mapsto1
\]
acts by
\[
  \Phi_n(r\cdot C)=\Phi_n(C)+(n-1)! \pmod{n!},
\]
and the reversal of the indices acts by
\[
  \Phi_n(s\cdot C)=a-\Phi_n(C) \pmod{n!}
\]
for some constant \(a\).

Thus the natural dihedral subgroup \(D_n\subset S_n\), acting on the indices
\(1,\ldots,n\), is realized as the ordinary dihedral symmetry of the cycle of
length \(n!\) formed by all chambers.
